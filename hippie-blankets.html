How do blankets keep you warm?

Before we get into the details of how many blankets you will need, let's talk about how blankets keep your body warm. Let's dive into the science behind blankets.

Blankets are not capable of creating heat themselves. You can't feel heat radiating from blankets unless they are heated. You are the one who makes the blanket warm.

Human body radiates heat constantly that warms the air around it. Layers of fabric around your body trap heat and keep you warm. To let the hot air out, we have a trick that allows you to stick your leg out of the blankets when it gets too hot.

What number of blankets do I need?

Benefits of Using More Blankets

It's okay to have more than one blanket in your bed. You can reap many unique benefits from having more blankets.

  • Additional Weight: Extra blankets can feel almost as if they are hugging you and cuddling you while you sleep. You can relax by adding weight to your blankets. This is why weighted blankets have become so popular.
  • Security: A blanket can be used to provide additional security and weight. This was something that we adopted as children, because it was the only place where we could hide from the monsters in our closets.
  • Extra warmth: A blanket can be a blanket. This is ideal for people in colder climates who don't get warm at night.

Benefits of Using Fewer Blankets

It's okay to sleep with less, of course.

  • Avoid Overheating: There is nothing worse than being hot in bed. You don't need to worry about overheating or trapping heat in your bed. You will be able to rest in cool and breezy environments.
  • It's easy to tidy up: If you have fewer blankets, making your bed is much easier. You can no longer fold extra blankets away when you aren't using them.
  • Pajama-Friendly: Wearing pajamas is easier with fewer blankets. Extra layers can make it easy to heat up, so only one blanket will keep you comfortable.

Blankets by the Seasons

It is a smart idea to adjust your blanket strategy according to the changing seasons. You might choose to pile on blankets in winter, and then store them away when it gets below 70 degrees at night. If you don't like the weight of several blankets during winter, you can get a single heavier blanket in the summer to make up the difference.
